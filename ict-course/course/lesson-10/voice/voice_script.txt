A common pattern in markets is this:
- price moves strongly in one direction
- then price returns
- but instead of continuing, it **fails** to make the next expected structure move

ICT names a particular form of this failure as a **Mitigation Block**.

The book frames it as:
- “a failure of price to mark the higher high or lower low”
- “failure of price to swing higher/lower”

---

From the book:

Mitigation block in ICT is a reversal pattern indicated by:
- failure of price to mark the higher high or lower low
- failure to continue the current market trend
- failure to break previous market structure

After the shift in market structure, you can mark the failed price move as:
- a level of support or resistance

**Key translation to teach clearly:**  
Mitigation = price “comes back” and forms a meaningful level after a failure.

---

Teach the bearish sequence exactly as described:

**When it forms:**
- At the end of a **bullish trend**
- Price reaches a strong bearish institutional reference point such as:
  - bearish order block
  - breaker block
  - higher timeframe buy side liquidity

**What you expect to happen:**
- Instead of making a new **higher high**, price makes a **lower high**

Then:
- price declines
- breaks the previous **higher low**
- creates a market structure shift to the down side

This is called:
> “failure of price to swing higher”

1) Price makes a **Higher High (HH)**
2) Pullback creates a **Higher Low (HL)**
3) The HL fails to produce a new higher high
4) Price breaks below HL (break of structure)
5) A lower high / bearish shift confirms the mitigation concept

**Most important detail from the book:**
- “the last down close candlestick before the rally is considered the most reactive and often marked as the mitigation block.”

So on your chart, don’t overcomplicate—anchor your mitigation zone around the **last reactive close candle**.

---

Mirror logic from the book:

**When it forms:**
- At the end of a bearish trend
- Price reaches a strong bullish institutional reference point such as:
  - bullish order block
  - breaker block
  - higher timeframe sell side liquidity

**What you expect:**
- Instead of making a new **lower low**, price forms a **higher low**

Then:
- price rises
- breaks the previous **lower high**
- shifts market structure to the upside

This is:
> “failure of price to swing lower”

1) Price forms a **Lower Low (LL)**
2) Pullback creates a **Lower High (LH)**
3) LH fails to drive price lower
4) Price breaks above LH
5) Market structure shifts up

**Again, anchor precision:**
- the last bullish up-close candle at LH becomes the mitigation block (most reactive).

---

For this critical-path course:
- Mitigation blocks are a **reversal / failure level concept**
- They become actionable when combined with:
  - liquidity context,
  - PD-Array premium/discount,
  - and structure shift confirmation.
