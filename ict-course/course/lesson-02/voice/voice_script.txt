In the market, not every price is equally valuable.  
ICT uses a simple idea to structure your decisions:

- **Sell where price is “expensive”** (premium)
- **Buy where price is “cheap”** (discount)

The tool that helps you do that is called the **PD-Array**.

---

**ICT PD-Array** stands for **“Premium and Discount Arrangement.”**  
It’s used like a **checklist** to determine potential trade entry points.

The PD-Array helps you find the **optimal price** to buy or sell:
- As a **seller**, you generally want to sell at **premium**
- As a **buyer**, you generally want to buy at **discount**

So instead of guessing, you structure your analysis around where price is relative to equilibrium.

---

To identify premium and discount zones, the book shows that you:
1. Take your swing range (an old high to an old low)
2. Plot Fibonacci with these key inputs:
   - (I) **1** = Start
   - (II) **0.5** = **Equilibrium**
   - (III) **0** = End

Then interpret the Fibonacci:
- The **0.5 level** is the **equilibrium**
- **Above 0.5** = **Premium zone**
- **Below 0.5** = **Discount zone**

**Important callout:**  
When you see price above equilibrium, focus on sell-side opportunities.  
When price is below equilibrium, focus on buy-side opportunities.

---

Use this simple checklist:

1) Identify the **dealing range** (old high to old low)  
2) Plot Fibonacci and locate **0.5 equilibrium**  
3) Decide bias:
   - Above 0.5 → premium (watch for sells)
   - Below 0.5 → discount (watch for buys)
4) Look for PD-Array structures:
   - Order Block / Breaker Block / FVG / etc.
5) Confirm with price delivery logic and execution levels
