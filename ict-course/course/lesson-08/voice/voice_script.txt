Sometimes price does something that looks “messy”—long wicks, lots of rejection, and then suddenly structure shifts.

ICT interprets that “mess” as evidence of liquidity being targeted and rejected.

That evidence is organized into a **Rejection Block**.

---

From the book:

- “ICT rejection block setup is based on the rejection wicks and liquidity sweep.”
- “When price sweeps old highs or lows liquidity forming long rejection wicks and shifting market structure, the long rejection wicks are termed as rejection block.”

So your mindset:
- liquidity sweep happens
- rejection wick forms
- market structure shifts
- mark the wick = rejection block

---

The bullish sequence (teach it step-by-step exactly like this):

1) **Sweep old lows**
   - Price sweeps sell-side liquidity (old lows)
2) **Form a low with long wick(s)**
   - Those long rejection wicks are your bullish rejection block
3) **Shift market structure to the buy side**
   - After the wick rejection, structure flips upward
4) **Execution trigger (entry logic)**
   - “When price reaches down below the body of the lowest open/closed candle of rejection block (to run sell stops) you can execute a buy trade.”

**How to explain to learners:**
- The rejection block marks the “institutional reaction zone.”
- Then price often retraces down briefly to run remaining sell stops.
- The buy trigger happens after that liquidity run.

---

Opposite logic:

1) **Sweep old highs**
   - Price sweeps buy-side liquidity (old highs)
2) **Form a high with long wick(s)**
   - The long upper rejection wick is the bearish rejection block
3) **Shift market structure to the sell side**
   - After the rejection, structure flips downward
4) **Execution trigger (entry logic)**
   - “When price reaches up above the body of highest open/closed candle of rejection block (to run buy stops) you can execute a sell trade.”

**Teach the symmetry clearly:**
- Bullish rejection → run sell stops → buy
- Bearish rejection → run buy stops → sell

---

On any chart replay:

1) Locate a liquidity sweep (old highs/lows)
2) Confirm long rejection wicks appear
3) Confirm structure shift direction
4) Mark the wick as the rejection block zone
5) Wait for the stop-run retracement into the block
6) Execute according to bias (buy or sell)
