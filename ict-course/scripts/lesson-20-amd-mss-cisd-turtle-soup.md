## Lesson Script

### Introduction — The Core Essentials

These four concepts — AMD, MSS, CISD, and Turtle Soup — are the bread and butter of ICT trading. They are not standalone strategies. They are building blocks. Once you understand them, you will start seeing the same institutional footprint play out on every timeframe, every day, in every market.

### ICT AMD Pattern — Accumulation, Manipulation, Distribution

AMD stands for Accumulation, Manipulation, and Distribution. It is the three-phase cycle that institutional order flow follows.

**Phase 1 — Accumulation**
The market opens, and price moves sideways near the opening price. It looks undecided. Price appears trapped inside a horizontal range. That is exactly what smart money wants you to think. During this phase, institutions are quietly building their positions. Retail traders see a clean support and resistance zone, so they place buy orders at the support line and sell orders at the resistance line. They put their stop-losses above the range high and below the range low. It looks like a safe, range-bound trade. It is not.

**Phase 2 — Manipulation**
Now smart money moves price in the opposite direction of their true intent. On a bearish day, they push price up — above the accumulation range — to trigger buy-stops and trap breakout buyers. On a bullish day, they push price down — below the accumulation range — to trigger sell-stops and trap breakout sellers. This phase is a false breakout. It is designed to shake out the weak hands and build liquidity for the real move.

**Phase 3 — Distribution**
Once the manipulation is complete, price reverses and moves hard in the actual intended direction. Smart money distributes their positions into the hands of latecomers. The breakout traders who jumped in during the manipulation are now trapped on the wrong side. Price does not return to give them an exit.

That is the AMD cycle. Spot the accumulation range, wait for the manipulation, and enter on the distribution.

### Market Structure Shift — MSS

A Market Structure Shift is your first warning that the trend is about to change. It can be short-term or long-term. MSS is defined by two things: a break of structure and a displacement move.

If price has been making higher highs and higher lows, that is an uptrend. When price breaks below the most recent swing low and follows through with a strong bearish candle — that displacement is the MSS. The old uptrend is now at risk.

The same logic applies in reverse. In a downtrend of lower highs and lower lows, price breaks above the most recent swing high. The displacement candle confirms the shift. The downtrend is over — or at least pausing.

MSS is a signal, not an entry. You wait for MSS, then go to a lower timeframe to look for a retracement into a PD Array. That retracement is your entry.

### Change in the State of Delivery — CISD

CISD is a more specific confirmation signal. It tells you that the direction of price delivery has changed. If price was being delivered to the buy-side — meaning candles were closing higher and taking out highs — a CISD would occur when that delivery flips to the sell-side.

The rule is simple: On a bearish delivery, CISD is confirmed when price closes above the opening price of the bearish move. On a bullish delivery, CISD is confirmed when price closes below the opening price of the bullish move.

Think of it as a line in the sand. As long as price stays on one side of that line, the current delivery is intact. The moment it closes on the other side, the state of delivery has changed. Use CISD alongside MSS for stronger confirmation. When you see both together, the reversal signal is much more reliable.

### Turtle Soup — Liquidity Sweep at Support and Resistance

The Turtle Soup pattern is all about hunting stop-losses. It works best in ranging markets where price oscillates between a known support and a known resistance level.

Most traders mark a breakout above resistance or below support as a continuation signal. They buy the breakout above resistance. They sell the breakout below support. ICT looks at it differently. ICT says that breakout is a trap — a run on stops.

Price moves for two reasons only: to balance an imbalance and to hunt liquidity. When price breaks above resistance, it is not signaling a new uptrend. It is running the buy-stops sitting above that resistance. Once those stops are collected, price reverses and drops.

Same thing at support. Price breaks below, sweeps the sell-stops, and reverses back up.

The Turtle Soup trade is simple: wait for the false breakout, wait for the reversal confirmation — usually an MSS or CISD on a lower timeframe — and then enter in the opposite direction. Your stop goes beyond the sweep level. Your target is the opposite side of the range.

**Question 1**
You see price chop sideways for two hours near the open. Then price spikes above the range high with a strong candle, and the next candle closes back inside the range. The daily bias is bearish. What AMD phase just ended, and what is likely coming next?

**Question 2**
Price has been making lower highs and lower lows all session. Suddenly, price breaks above the most recent swing high with a large displacement candle. On the next candle, price closes below the opening price of that bullish move. Which two confirmations did you just get, and what is your next step?

**Question 3**
In a ranging market, price breaks below a well-known support level, drops two candles, then immediately reverses and closes back above support. A 5-minute CISD confirms the flip. Do you enter long or short? Where is your stop and what is your target?

## Recap

- AMD Pattern: Accumulation (range), Manipulation (false breakout), Distribution (real move).
- MSS is a break of swing point with displacement — a trend reversal signal.
- CISD confirms the reversal — price closes on the opposite side of the delivery open.
- Turtle Soup is a false breakout at support or resistance that hunts stop-losses before reversing.
- Use MSS and CISD together for higher-probability entry confirmation.
- Price moves to balance imbalances and hunt liquidity — nothing else.
