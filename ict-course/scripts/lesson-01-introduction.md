### 5) Why traders fail (6:30–8:00)
A trader can fail even with solid understanding of rules.  
The book explains that failure often comes from:
- **lack of knowledge**, and also
- **overload of unnecessary and scattered information**

So this course is built to remove the overload and focus on essential ICT fundamentals.

### 6) What is ICT trading? (8:00–9:40)
High-level definition:
- ICT (Inner Circle Trader) trading is a methodology associated with Michael J. Huddleston.
- The core emphasis is on the **relationship between time and price**.
- Price delivery is tied to a concept of liquidity targeting through the **IPDA** (Interbank Price Delivery Algorithm).

**Two main reasons for price delivery:**
1. **Balancing an imbalance in price**
2. **Hunting liquidity**

### 7) ICT vs Price Action (9:40–11:30)
If you previously traded “price action,” you might ask: what makes ICT different?

The book’s argument:
- Price action may not provide a clear rationale for why price moves.
- ICT provides a **logical framework** for price delivery.
- This logic supports better decision accuracy and a higher win ratio—especially by grounding your entries in liquidity + time.

### 8) Road Map: how this course is structured (11:30–12:30)
This course follows a step-by-step learning journey with phases:

- **Phase 1:** Core ICT concepts (PD-arrays & dealing range)
- **Phase 2:** Market profiles and advanced structure
- **Phase 3:** Timing & execution strategies (sessions, kill zones, entry models)
- **Phase 4:** Risk management & trading psychology
