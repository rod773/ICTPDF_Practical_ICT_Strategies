## Lesson Script

### 1) Hook: Why Order Blocks are foundational (0:00–1:30)
Order Blocks are one of the first structures you learn in ICT because they represent areas where large institutional activity is believed to be concentrated.

In simple terms:
- An **Order Block** is a zone of institutional interest.
- It can signal **reversal** or **continuation**, depending on context.

---

### 2) Definition (1:30–3:00)
From the book:

> “ICT order block is basically an area on the price chart which indicates the huge institutional orders and signals the strong reversal or continuation of price.”

So your job is to recognize *the candle sequence* that created the strong impulse that follows.

---

### 3) Bullish Order Block (3:00–8:30)
A **bullish OB** is described like this:

- It is typically the **last bearish candle** before the bullish impulse
- It usually consists of **two candles**
  - Candle 1: bearish
  - Candle 2: bullish impulse begins

#### Conditions for a valid bullish OB
You validate it with these checks:

1) **Second candle grabs the low**
   - The second candle must be bullish
   - It should **grab the low** of the previous bearish candle
   - Price should go **below** the previous bearish low

2) **Second candle closes above the previous bearish high**
   - The bullish candle should **close above** the high of the prior bearish candle

3) **Imbalance in the lower time frame**
   - In the OB zone, you want to see imbalance on a lower timeframe

4) **Structure shift in lower timeframe**
   - Confirm an ICT **market structure shift** on the lower timeframe

#### Candle engulfing rule (high precision)
The book’s emphasis:
- The second candle should **completely engulf** the first candle:
  - body to body
  - wick to wick

#### Extra precision note (Mentorship idea)
The book mentions a “consecutive candles” approach, but then clarifies sensitivity:
- Use the last sensitive bearish candle as the preferred OB for accuracy.

**To say on camera:**  
“Order blocks are not just ‘any bearish candle.’ They’re the *last bearish candle* that gets engulfed and triggers the bullish impulse.”

---

### 4) Bearish Order Block (8:30–14:00)
A **bearish OB** is the opposite setup:

- It is typically the **last bullish candle** before the bearish impulse move
- Usually consists of two candles:
  - Candle 1: bullish
  - Candle 2: bearish impulse begins

#### Conditions for a valid bearish OB
1) **Second candle grabs the high**
   - The second candle must be bearish
   - It should **grab the high** of the prior bullish candle
   - Price should go **above** the previous bullish high

2) **Second candle closes below the previous bullish low**
   - The bearish candle closes **below** the low of the prior bullish candle

3) **Imbalance on lower timeframe**
   - Look for imbalance inside the OB region

4) **ICT Market Structure Shift (lower timeframe)**
   - Confirm structure shift to the downside

#### Bearish engulfing rule
Again, precision matters:
- Candle 2 should engulf Candle 1:
  - body + wick engulfing

#### Precision note from the mentorship
Same idea:
- Even if there are multiple consecutive bullish candles,
- the **last bullish candle** is often the most sensitive and preferred.

---

### 5) Micro-Workflow: how to find OBs quickly (14:00–17:00)
Use this workflow:

1) Identify the **impulse move**
2) Go backwards to find the **last opposing candle**
3) Check bullish/bearish OB rules
4) Confirm:
   - engulfing
   - imbalance on lower timeframe
   - market structure shift
