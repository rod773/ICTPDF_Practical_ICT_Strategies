## Lesson Script

### 1) Hook: The strategy that yields 20–30 pips every day (0:00–1:30)

Michael Huddleston once said, "To quit your job, you need something that repeats every day and yields five handles."

He was talking about the ICT Silver Bullet.

The Silver Bullet is a time-based algorithmic trading model that happens three times a day in a one-hour window. It repeats every single trading day and can yield 20 to 30 pips per session. That's three opportunities per day to take a high-probability scalp based on liquidity and fair value gaps.

If you want consistency, this is it.

---

### 2) How the ICT Silver Bullet Works (1:30–4:30)

Price moves for two reasons: to balance imbalance, or to take liquidity. The Silver Bullet combines both.

Here's the setup process:

**Before the session starts**, mark the nearest buy-side and sell-side liquidity on the 15-minute timeframe. Buy-side liquidity sits above old highs — that's where stop losses of shorts and breakout buyers cluster. Sell-side liquidity sits below old lows — where stop losses of longs and breakout sellers wait.

Now you have your targets.

**What to expect:**
- If price has already swept liquidity on one side, it may reverse and go grab the other side.
- If price has run above or below a liquidity level and kept going, it may continue further in that direction.

**When the Silver Bullet session starts**, drop to a lower timeframe — 1-minute or 3-minute — and look for an ICT Market Structure Shift (MSS) in the direction of your next draw on liquidity. That MSS tells you smart money just committed.

After the MSS, look for an ICT Fair Value Gap behind the move. Use the ICT Premium and Discount tool to find the best FVG — ideally one that sits in the discount zone for longs or premium zone for shorts.

When price trades back into that FVG, you execute. It's a scalp trade — in and out. No holding, no hoping.

---

### 3) ICT Silver Bullet Times (4:30–6:00)

The Silver Bullet fires three times a day, each session lasting roughly one hour. The exact times depend on your time zone, but here are the standard windows:

**London Silver Bullet:** Opens at the start of the London session — high volatility, institutional order flow entering the market.

**New York AM Silver Bullet:** Opens at the start of the New York session — overlaps with London, maximum liquidity.

**New York PM Silver Bullet:** Opens later in the New York session — often catches the afternoon rebalancing.

Each one follows the same rules: mark your liquidity on the 15-minute chart, wait for the session to start, look for MSS on a lower timeframe, find the FVG behind it, and scalp when price returns to the gap.

---

### 4) ICT Kill Zones — Overview (6:00–7:30)

Kill Zones are time windows with high trading volume and volatility. They are based on the four primary trading sessions: Sydney, Tokyo, London, and New York.

There are four Kill Zones you need to know:
1. Asian Kill Zone
2. London Kill Zone
3. New York Kill Zone
4. London Close Kill Zone

Each one has its own personality — specific pairs to trade, specific volatility levels, and specific types of setups you can expect.

---

### 5) ICT Asian Kill Zone — 7:00 PM – 10:00 PM NY Time (7:30–9:00)

The Asian session is the quietest of the four. Volume is low, ranges are tight, and the market is mostly consolidating.

**Key traits:**
- Based on the Asian range
- Least volatility of all sessions
- USD typically consolidates during this time
- Best pairs: AUD, NZD, JPY — the Asia-Pacific currencies

**What to look for:**
The Asian open can set up a 15 to 20 pips scalp trade. You're looking for the same Silver Bullet principles — liquidity and FVG — but within the tighter Asian range. Don't expect huge moves. Take what the market gives you and move on.

---

### 6) ICT London Kill Zone — 2:00 AM – 5:00 AM NY Time (9:00–10:30)

This is where things get real.

The London session has the highest trading volume compared to Asia. European banks are open, institutions are moving money, and the market wakes up.

**Key traits:**
- Highest probability of a large directional move
- Hot pairs: EUR, GBP — the European currencies
- Much higher volume than the Asian session

**What to look for:**
The London open can offer 30+ pips directional trades. On a bullish day, price sets the high of the day during the London Kill Zone. On a bearish day, price sets the low of the day. That means the direction established in London often sets the tone for the entire day.

If you're only trading one session, this is the one.

---

### 7) ICT New York Kill Zone — 7:00 AM – 10:00 AM NY Time (10:30–12:00)

The New York session overlaps with London for several hours. That overlap is the most volatile period of the trading day.

**Key traits:**
- Highly volatile due to London-NY overlap
- USD pairs are the center of attention
- Massive institutional order flow

**What to look for:**
The New York session often retraces back into the London trading range and sets up an Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) pattern. When that happens, you can get 30 to 40 pips per trade. The combination of two major financial centers trading at the same time creates the deepest liquidity pool of the day.

---

### 8) ICT London Close Kill Zone — 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM NY Time (12:00–13:30)

As London traders head home, they close their positions. That creates a specific type of price action.

**Key traits:**
- Most traders secure positions before the close of the London day
- Price retraces back to the daily range
- Lower volatility than the London open, but still tradable

**What to look for:**
The London Close can offer a 5-minute OTE setup with a 15 to 20 pips scalp. It's not as explosive as the London open or the New York open, but it's consistent. The retracement into the daily range creates clean patterns that repeat day after day.

---

### 9) Putting It All Together (13:30–15:00)

Here's how you combine Silver Bullet with Kill Zones:

1. **Know the time.** Check which Kill Zone is active. That tells you which pairs to watch and what kind of move to expect.
2. **Mark liquidity.** Before the Silver Bullet window opens, mark the nearest buy-side and sell-side liquidity on the 15-minute chart.
3. **Wait for MSS.** When the session starts, drop to 1-minute or 3-minute and wait for a Market Structure Shift in the direction of the next liquidity target.
4. **Find the FVG.** Look for a fair value gap behind the MSS. Use Premium/Discount to confirm it's in a good zone.
5. **Execute on the retrace.** When price comes back to the FVG, take the trade. Scalp 20–30 pips and move on.

Three times a day. Every day. That's the Silver Bullet.
