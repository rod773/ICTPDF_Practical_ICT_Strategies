## Lesson Script

---

### 1) Hook: The two opening models you can trade before 09:00 AM (0:00–1:30)

Michael Huddleston dedicated an entire bonus lecture to two time-based models that happen before most traders even pour their first coffee — the 08:00 AM Model and the 07:00 AM Model.

These are not vague concepts. They are precise, repeatable algorithms based on how institutions manipulate price in the pre-session window. If you sit down before 08:30 AM New York time, you have two structured ways to enter the market using Relative Equal Highs, Relative Equal Lows, Fair Value Gaps, and Breaker Blocks.

This lesson walks you through both models, their key elements, and how to apply them step by step.

---

### 2) The Building Blocks You Need (1:30–3:00)

Both models share a common vocabulary. Before we dive into the specific times, let's define the concepts you will use in every setup.

**Relative Equal High (REH):** A high that has a lower swing high on the right side of it. It forms because of a swing failure — price tried to push higher, could not, and is now showing you a potential reversal point.

**Relative Equal Low (REL):** A low that has a higher swing low on the right side of it. Same logic — price failed to push lower and is setting up for a reversal.

**Displacement:** A strong counter-move against a pre-session, pre-day, or pre-price swing. It tells you smart money just entered.

**ICT Order Block (OB):** The zone where large orders were executed by institutions before the move began.

**Breaker Block:** When price sweeps the liquidity of a low or a Relative Equal Low by dropping below it, then reverses and breaks above the previous swing high. The old support becomes resistance — that zone is the breaker.

**SIBI (Sell-side Imbalance Buyside Inefficiency):** A down-closed ICT Fair Value Gap. Think of it as a bearish FVG that price may retrace into on the way down.

**BISI (Buyside Imbalance Sell-side Inefficiency):** An up-closed ICT Fair Value Gap. A bullish FVG that price may retrace into on the way up.

Every one of these will appear in both models. If you are unsure about any of them, review the earlier lessons on Order Blocks, Breaker Blocks, and Fair Value Gaps.

---

### 3) The 08:00 AM Model — Overview (3:00–4:30)

The 08:00 AM Model focuses on what happens in the window just after 08:00 AM New York local time. The key rule: sit at your computer before 08:30 AM, and preferably by 08:00 AM.

**Timeframes:** Only three — 15-minute, 5-minute, and 1-minute.

**The 15-minute chart** is your parent chart. It gives you your bias and shows you the important levels like inefficiencies and draws on liquidity.

**When the clock hits 08:00 AM**, you drop down to the 1-minute timeframe and watch for a Relative Equal High or a Relative Equal Low to form. That is the trigger.

Once the REH or REL forms, you wait for price to grab it — meaning price sweeps through that level, taking out the stops sitting beyond it. That sweep is the hunt. After the hunt, you watch for price to return back into the range.

---

### 4) 08:00 AM Model — Bearish Scenario (4:30–6:00)

Let's walk through a bearish example step by step.

**Step 1:** Price forms a Relative Equal High on the 1-minute chart. You mark it.

**Step 2:** Price continues up and grabs the REH — it sweeps above it, taking out buy-side liquidity.

**Step 3:** Now you wait. Price returns back into the original range and closes below the swing low. That close below the swing low is your Market Structure Shift — MSS. The bias is now bearish.

**Step 4:** Mark three things in the drop:
- The bearish Order Block — the last bullish candle before the drop began.
- The SIBI — any down-closed FVG formed during the drop.
- The bearish Breaker Block — if price swept a low or REL and then broke down.

**Step 5:** When price retraces back up to any of those three zones — the OB, the SIBI, or the Breaker Block — you execute a sell.

**Target:** The next draw on liquidity to the downside — either a Relative Equal Low or the low of the previous session.

---

### 5) 08:00 AM Model — Bullish Scenario (6:00–7:30)

Same structure, flipped for a bullish move.

**Step 1:** Price forms a Relative Equal Low on the 1-minute chart.

**Step 2:** Price continues down and grabs the REL — it sweeps below it, taking out sell-side liquidity.

**Step 3:** Wait for price to return back into the range and close above the swing high. That close above the swing high is your bullish MSS.

**Step 4:** Mark three things in the rise:
- The bullish Order Block — the last bearish candle before the rise.
- The BISI — any up-closed FVG formed during the rise.
- The bullish Breaker Block — if price swept a high or an REH and then broke up.

**Step 5:** When price retraces back down to any of those three zones, you execute a buy.

**Target:** The next draw on liquidity to the upside — either a Relative Equal High or the high of the previous session.

The whole model revolves around the same sequence: form the REL or REH, let it get grabbed, wait for the MSS on the return, mark your zones, and enter on the retrace.

---

### 6) The 07:00 AM Model — Overview (7:30–9:00)

The 07:00 AM Model is similar but starts an hour earlier, and it adds a few unique elements.

**Key Elements:** SIBI, BISI, IFVG, Breaker Block, Relative Equal High, Relative Equal Low, Fractal, and Consequent Encroachment.

**The most critical concept here is the IFVG — the Implied Fair Value Gap.** Specifically, the very first FVG that formed before the stop hunt. That one is the most sensitive and the most important.

**Key Times:** 07:00 AM, 08:00 AM, and 09:00 AM New York local time.

**Key Timeframes:** 15-minute, 5-minute, 1-minute, and 15-seconds.

The process starts at 07:00 AM. If you are brand new to the chart at that moment, do not look at anything that happened before. Start fresh.

---

### 7) 07:00 AM Model — The Setup (9:00–10:30)

**Step 1:** At 07:00 AM, look for a Relative Equal High or Relative Equal Low to form on the 5-minute or 1-minute timeframe.

**Step 2:** Wait for price to grab it — sweep above the REH or below the REL.

**Step 3:** After the grab, price will return and take out the swing point, giving you your MSS.

Now here is where the 07:00 AM Model differs from the 08:00 AM Model.

Instead of marking the Order Block as your primary entry zone, you go looking for the very first FVG that appeared before the stop hunt. That first FVG is your Implied FVG — IFVG. It is the most sensitive level because it represents the original imbalance that started the move.

You also mark the Breaker Block on the same side.

**Bearish entry:** Price grabs the REH, returns below the swing low. Mark the first FVG before the stop hunt as your IFVG, plus the bearish Breaker Block. You execute a sell at the Consequent Encroachment of the IFVG — that is the 50% retracement level of the FVG. If there is no IFVG, use the Breaker Block instead.

**Bullish entry:** Price grabs the REL, returns above the swing high. Mark the IFVG and the bullish Breaker Block. Execute a buy at the Consequent Encroachment of the IFVG. If no IFVG, use the Breaker Block.

**Fibonacci inputs for this model:** 0, 0.5, 1, -2, -2.5. You can use these to measure Consequent Encroachment and identify extended targets.

---

### 8) Important Note on Pre-Session Behavior (10:30–11:30)

There is a critical observation from ICT that applies to both models.

After 07:00 AM, 08:00 AM, and 09:00 AM, in the first 30 minutes of that hour — the pre-session range — you should expect the opposite price movement to the relative trend.

What does that mean in practice?

If the overall bias or the recent trend is bullish, the first 30 minutes after the hour are likely to be bearish. If the trend is bearish, the first 30 minutes are likely to be bullish.

This is the market's way of creating a false start, shaking out retail traders who jump in too early. Do not fight it. Anticipate the counter-move, wait for it to play out, and then enter in the direction of the MSS once the REH or REL is grabbed and price returns.

---

### 9) Putting It All Together (11:30–12:30)

Here is your daily workflow if you want to trade these models:

**06:50 AM** — Open your charts. Set your timeframes: 15-minute, 5-minute, 1-minute, and 15-second.

**07:00 AM** — Start watching the 5-minute and 1-minute for a Relative Equal High or Relative Equal Low. Do not look at prior data if you are new to the session.

**Once REH or REL forms** — Wait for price to grab it. Mark the first FVG before the stop hunt as your IFVG.

**After the MSS** — Mark your Breaker Block. Enter at the Consequent Encroachment of the IFVG, or at the Breaker Block if there is no clear IFVG.

**08:00 AM** — Repeat the same process, but now you can also use the Order Block, SIBI, and BISI as entry zones on the 1-minute. The 15-minute chart gives you your bias and your liquidity targets.

**09:00 AM** — One more opportunity. Same structure.

In all three windows, the first 30 minutes of the hour will likely move opposite to the trend. Let that counter-move happen. Do not front-run it. Wait for the full sequence — REH or REL, grab, return, MSS, entry zone, retrace, execute.

## Practice / Assignment

1. **Map the 08:00 AM sequence on a live chart.** For the next three trading days, sit down at 08:00 AM NY time. On the 1-minute chart, identify the REH or REL that forms first. Note whether it was grabbed, whether price returned for an MSS, and whether you could mark a clear OB, SIBI, or Breaker Block. Write one sentence per day describing what happened.

2. **Identify the IFVG before a stop hunt.** During one 07:00 AM session, scroll back on the 1-minute or 15-second chart right after price grabs the REH or REL. Find the very first FVG that appeared before the sweep. Mark it on your chart and note the price level of its 50% Consequent Encroachment. Check whether price later retraced to that level.

3. **Paper trade one entry per session.** For each of the three windows (07:00 AM, 08:00 AM, 09:00 AM), paper trade one setup following the full model: wait for REH/REL, grab, MSS, mark your entry zone, and execute on the retrace. Log the entry price, stop loss, and target. After five trading days, calculate your win rate separately for the 07:00 AM Model and the 08:00 AM Model.

## Recap

- The 08:00 AM Model uses REH/REL on the 1-minute chart, waits for the grab, then marks OB, SIBI, or Breaker Block after the MSS — execute on retrace
- The 07:00 AM Model uses the same REH/REL structure but focuses on the first FVG before the stop hunt (IFVG) as the primary entry zone
- Consequent Encroachment (50% retracement of the IFVG) is the specific entry level for the 07:00 AM Model
- Key timeframes: 15m (bias and parent chart), 5m and 1m (execution), 15s (optional for IFVG precision)
- Key times: 07:00 AM, 08:00 AM, and 09:00 AM NY local time
- In the first 30 minutes of each hour, expect price to move opposite to the relative trend
