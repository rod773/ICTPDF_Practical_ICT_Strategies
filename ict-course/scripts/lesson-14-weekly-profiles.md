## Lesson Script

---

**[0:00–1:00] — Introduction**

What if I told you that the market tends to follow certain scripts throughout the trading week? Not rigidly — but there are recognizable rhythms. ICT calls these **Weekly Profiles**.

They're not predictions. They're **frameworks** — patterns of price behavior that repeat often enough to give you an edge. Once you understand them, you can stop guessing and start anticipating.

In this lesson we'll cover all 8 classic weekly profiles — the bullish and bearish versions for Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday. And most importantly, I'll show you how to anticipate which one is in play using higher timeframe premium and discount arrays.

---

**[1:00–2:00] — What Is a Weekly Profile?**

A weekly profile is a conceptual framework that describes how price typically behaves across the trading week — Monday through Friday. Each profile has a **low of the week** or a **high of the week** that gets formed on a specific day.

The key insight: the market often *manipulates* early in the week before making its real move. Understanding this helps you avoid getting faked out on Monday or Tuesday.

All profiles revolve around the idea of price either reaching into a **higher timeframe discount array** (for bullish profiles) or a **higher timeframe premium array** (for bearish profiles).

---

**[2:00–4:00] — Classic Tuesday Low of the Week (Bullish)**

Let's start with the Tuesday Low profile.

When price is **bullish** on the higher timeframe, the market may manipulate on Monday — meaning it might rally or chop around. But notice this: it **hovers above a higher timeframe discount array**. It doesn't dip into it on Monday.

Then on **Tuesday**, it finally drops into that discount array to mark the **low of the week**. From there, price reverses and trends higher for the rest of the week.

**How to anticipate it:** Know your higher timeframe discount array. If the market fails to drop into it on Monday — if it stays above it or chops sideways above it — Tuesday is very likely the day. Watch the **London and New York sessions** on Tuesday for that sweep into discount.

This works because Monday's action was just a manipulation to trap late bulls or confuse traders. The real move happens Tuesday.

---

**[4:00–5:30] — Classic Tuesday High of the Week (Bearish)**

This is the mirror image.

When price is **bearish** on the higher timeframe, Monday may show a manipulation rally or chop, but price stays **below the higher timeframe premium array**. It doesn't reach it.

Then on **Tuesday**, price rises into that premium array to mark the **high of the week**. After that, it reverses and trends lower.

**How to anticipate it:** Same logic in reverse. If Monday stays below the premium array and doesn't tag it, expect Tuesday to be the day that sweeps up into premium and reverses. That Tuesday high is likely the high of the entire week.

---

**[5:30–7:00] — Classic Wednesday Low & High of the Week**

Now Wednesday. Slightly different rhythm.

**Wednesday Low (Bullish):** Monday sees a rally. Tuesday sees a retracement — it pulls back — but crucially, it **fails to mark the low of the week**. It doesn't reach the discount array. Then on **Wednesday**, price drops into the discount array to form the weekly low.

**Wednesday High (Bearish):** Monday drops. Tuesday bounces but doesn't reach the premium array — not yet. Then on **Wednesday**, price rises into premium to mark the weekly high.

The difference from Tuesday profiles: the retracement is delayed. Monday pushes one direction, Tuesday starts to retrace, and Wednesday finishes the job. You get an extra day of chop before the real move.

---

**[7:00–8:30] — Classic Thursday Low & High of the Week**

Thursday profiles are more nuanced. They involve the **previous week's range**.

**Thursday Low (Bullish):** Monday opens **inside** the previous week's range. Monday and Tuesday see a rally that pushes **above** the previous week's high. On Wednesday, price retraces — but not into discount. It stays above. Then on **Thursday**, it finally drops into the higher timeframe discount array to mark the low of the week.

The tell here is the open inside prior week range, followed by the push above prior week high early in the week. If that happens and Wednesday's retracement is shallow, Thursday is your day.

**Thursday High (Bearish):** Mirror — open inside prior week range, Monday and Tuesday drop below prior week low. Wednesday bounces but doesn't reach premium. Thursday pushes into premium for the weekly high.

---

**[8:30–10:00] — Classic Friday Low & High of the Week (Weekly Reversal)**

These are the reversals.

**Friday Low (Bullish/Reversal):** Monday drops into the discount array **early** — on Monday itself. Then Tuesday through Wednesday, price rallies. Thursday pushes into the **premium** array. And then on **Friday**, it drops all the way back to the discount array to mark the low of the week.

This is a full round trip. Monday discounts, rallies through midweek, tags premium on Thursday, then reverses hard into discount on Friday.

**Friday High (Bearish/Reversal):** Monday rallies into premium. Tuesday through Wednesday drops. Thursday pushes into discount. Friday reverses back up to premium to mark the high of the week.

These profiles are called **weekly reversals** for a reason — the high or low prints late in the week after price has already swept both sides.

---

**[10:00–12:00] — How to Anticipate Using PD-Arrays**

So how do you know which profile is likely?

The answer is in the **higher timeframe PD-Array**. Here's the process:

1. **Identify the higher timeframe trend.** Are we trading above or below the equilibrium — the 0.5 Fibonacci level — on your higher timeframe chart (daily or 4H)?

2. **If above equilibrium (bullish bias):** Look for bullish profiles — Tuesday Low, Wednesday Low, Thursday Low, or Friday Low. The market is likely to seek the discount array on those specific days.

3. **If below equilibrium (bearish bias):** Look for bearish profiles — Tuesday High, Wednesday High, Thursday High, or Friday High. Price will likely reach into the premium array.

4. **Narrow the day by observing early week action.** How does Monday behave?
   - Fails to reach discount on Monday? → Tuesday Low likely.
   - Rallies Monday, shallow Tuesday retrace? → Wednesday Low likely.
   - Inside prior week range, pushes above prior week high, shallow Wednesday retrace? → Thursday Low likely.
   - Hits discount on Monday, rallies to premium by Thursday? → Friday Low (reversal) likely.

Same logic works in reverse for bearish profiles.

---

**[12:00–13:00] — Practical Tips & Common Mistakes**

A few things to keep in mind:

**Don't force a profile.** The market won't always follow one perfectly. If Monday's action doesn't match any profile setup, stay flexible.

**Sessions matter.** Most weekly lows and highs happen during **London** or **New York** session. Pay attention to which session is active when price approaches the discount or premium array.

**Use multiple timeframes.** Weekly profiles work best when confirmed by higher timeframe PD-Arrays. Don't try to trade them on a 5-minute chart alone.

**The profile is a framework, not a prediction.** It gives you a probabilistic edge. Manage risk accordingly.

---

**[13:00–14:00] — Summary**

Let's recap the 8 profiles:

- **Tuesday Low/High** — Manipulation on Monday, the real move on Tuesday
- **Wednesday Low/High** — Monday pushes one way, Tuesday starts retracing, Wednesday finishes
- **Thursday Low/High** — Open inside prior week range, push beyond it early, shallow retrace Wednesday, move Thursday
- **Friday Low/High (Reversal)** — Sweep one side Monday, opposite side by Thursday, back to the first side Friday

The weekly profile model trains your eye to see the market's rhythm. Practice identifying which profile is playing out each week. Over time, you'll start to anticipate the moves before they happen.

## Recap

- ICT Weekly Profiles are 8 conceptual frameworks describing typical weekly price behavior — bullish and bearish versions for Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday.
- Each profile has a specific sequence of daily movements that culminates in a weekly low or high at a higher timeframe PD-Array level.
- Monday action is key to anticipation: if the discount or premium array is not reached on Monday, expect Tuesday to deliver it; if Monday hits discount and price later reaches premium by Thursday, expect a Friday reversal.
- The higher timeframe equilibrium (0.5 Fibonacci) determines the directional bias — above for bullish profiles, below for bearish.
- These are probabilistic frameworks, not rigid rules. Use them to develop a bias, not a certainty.
