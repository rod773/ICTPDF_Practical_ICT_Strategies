## Lesson Script

---

**[0:00–1:00] — Introduction**

So far we've looked at weekly profiles — how the market tends to behave across a full trading week. Now let's zoom in. What happens **inside** a single day?

ICT Intraday Profiles are conceptual frameworks that describe typical patterns of price behavior during specific times of a trading day. They're built around the idea that price tends to form its daily high during a sell day and its daily low during a buy day — and that these extremes often occur at predictable times.

In this lesson, we'll cover the Central Bank Dealers Range, the London Normal and Delayed Protraction profiles for both sell and buy scenarios, and how to apply these concepts to the New York session.

---

**[1:00–2:30] — Central Bank Dealers Range (CBDR)**

Let's start with a critical concept: the **Central Bank Dealers Range**, or CBDR.

The CBDR is an important central range of price that runs from **2:00 PM to 8:00 PM New York local time**. Think of it as a zone where institutional price discovery happens during those hours.

What makes the CBDR useful is that it can be replicated above or below using standard deviation — meaning you can project the range to identify potential price targets.

To measure the CBDR, take the high and low of that 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM window. You have a choice: you can measure using the wicks of the candlesticks, or you can use the bodies. ICT prefers **bodies over wicks**. The body measurement gives you a cleaner range that filters out the noise of wick extensions.

Once you have the height of the CBDR, you can project it to anticipate where price might reach on the following day or in subsequent sessions.

---

**[2:30–4:30] — London Normal Protraction Sell Profile**

Now let's talk about intraday profiles that involve the London session.

The **London Normal Protraction Sell Profile** unfolds in three phases:

**Phase I** — From midnight to 2:00 AM New York local time, price rallies up. This is the initial move.

**Phase II** — After 2:00 AM, price continues to rally but with **slow momentum**. This is the protraction phase — price is still moving up, but it's losing steam. The candles get smaller, the pace slows. This tells you buyers are running out of energy.

**Phase III** — At **5:00 AM New York local time**, price forms a high and the sell-off begins. That 5:00 AM turn is the trigger.

To trade this profile, you need two things:
1. A **higher timeframe bearish bias** — you should already be looking to sell on the daily or 4H chart.
2. Price needs to reach a **premium array on the higher timeframe** — that's your zone.

Once price reaches that premium array and the 5:00 AM high forms, wait for confirmation — a displacement, a market structure shift, or a breaker — then enter short. Target discount PD-arrays or sell-side liquidity below.

---

**[4:30–6:00] — London Delayed Protraction Sell Profile**

The **London Delayed Protraction Sell Profile** is a variation. The setup is different, but the result is the same — a sell-off after 5:00 AM.

Here's how it plays out:

**Phase I** — Instead of rallying, price initially **trades down** and forms a low at **2:00 AM** New York local time.

**Phase II** — From 2:00 AM to 5:00 AM, price **protracts higher** with slow momentum. Notice the difference from the normal profile: in the normal version, price was already up and kept grinding higher. Here, price had to drop first, then reverse and grind up.

**Phase III** — At 5:00 AM, price forms a high and the sell-off begins.

To trade this, you still need a bearish daily bias. The entry comes after the 5:00 AM high — you wait for price to return to a PD Array in the premium zone. Once it does, you enter short and target sell-side liquidity below.

The key difference from the normal profile is the initial drop. If you see price making a low at 2:00 AM and then slowly grinding up into 5:00 AM, you're watching a delayed protraction unfold.

---

**[6:00–7:30] — London Delayed Protraction Buy Profile**

The buy version is the mirror image.

In the **London Delayed Protraction Buy Profile**, price initially trades **up** — then drops into discount during the London session at 2:00 AM, forming a low. From 2:00 AM to 5:00 AM, it protracts higher — slowly grinding up with low momentum candles. After 5:00 AM, the real rally begins.

For buy profiles, the requirements flip:
- You need a **bullish bias** on the higher timeframe.
- Wait for price to reach a **discount PD Array**.
- Enter after confirmation — look for a market structure shift or displacement on the 1-minute or 5-minute chart.

Target buy-side liquidity above. The same 5:00 AM turn time applies, but this time it's a launch pad for the rally, not the start of a sell-off.

---

**[7:30–9:00] — New York Session Trading**

What about the New York session? The intraday profile logic applies here too, but with a different approach.

If price has already taken liquidity during the London session and moved away from that level, the New York session often sees a retracement followed by a continuation.

Here's a practical workflow:

1. Draw a **Fibonacci retracement** from the London swing low to the London swing high.
2. Wait for price to retrace and tap the **OTE (Optimal Trade Entry) levels** — typically the 0.618 to 0.79 Fibonacci zone.
3. Look for a **market structure shift on the 1-minute chart** at those OTE levels.
4. Once you have the MSS, enter in the direction of the London move.

This works because the New York session tends to respect the London range. If London pushed price one direction and then price retraces into the OTE zone during New York, that retracement is often the final pullback before continuation.

---

**[9:00–10:00] — Putting It All Together**

Let's walk through a practical scenario.

Say it's a bearish day on the daily chart — price is below the equilibrium. You're looking for a sell.

At midnight, price starts to rally. By 2:00 AM, it's still moving up. From 2:00 AM to 5:00 AM, the candles get smaller — momentum is slowing. This is the London Normal Protraction Sell Profile setting up.

You check your higher timeframe and see a premium array above. Price reaches it around 4:30 AM. At 5:00 AM, a high forms. You wait for a 1-minute displacement to the downside — a break of a recent swing low — and you enter short.

Your target: the discount array below, or the sell-side liquidity below the previous day's low.

The profile gave you the **when** — the 5:00 AM turn. The higher timeframe gave you the **where** — the premium array. The confirmation gave you the **trigger**.

That's the power of intraday profiles.

---

**[10:00–11:00] — Common Mistakes**

A few things to watch out for:

**Ignoring the higher timeframe bias.** The profiles are much more reliable when they align with the daily or 4H direction. Trying to sell a London profile when the daily trend is bullish is fighting the tide.

**Forcing the 5:00 AM turn.** Not every day has a clean turn at 5:00 AM. If the protraction phase doesn't look like slow momentum — if price is still charging hard at 5:00 AM — sit it out.

**Misidentifying the CBDR.** Remember to measure using candlestick bodies, not wicks. And don't apply standard deviation projections without confirming with other PD-Array concepts.

**Ignoring the delayed profiles.** Just because price drops at 2:00 AM doesn't mean the sell profile is invalid. The delayed protraction is a different entry point but the same 5:00 AM turn.

---

**[11:00–12:00] — Summary**

Let's recap what we covered:

- **Central Bank Dealers Range (CBDR)** — the 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM NY local time range, measured using candle bodies, projectable via standard deviation
- **London Normal Protraction Sell** — price rallies midnight to 2:00 AM, protracts 2:00 AM to 5:00 AM, sells off after 5:00 AM
- **London Delayed Protraction Sell** — price drops to low at 2:00 AM, protracts higher 2:00 AM to 5:00 AM, sells off after 5:00 AM
- **London Delayed Protraction Buy** — price rallies then drops to low at 2:00 AM, protracts higher to 5:00 AM, rallies after 5:00 AM
- **New York session** — use Fibonacci from London swing, wait for OTE retracement, confirm with 1-minute MSS

Intraday profiles give you a structured way to think about the trading day. They tell you when to expect the turn, where to look for entries, and how to align with the higher timeframe direction.

## Practice / Assignment

1. **CBDR measurement practice.** On a daily chart, identify the 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM NY local time range for 5 trading days. Measure the height using candle bodies only. Then project that range above and below — see if price reached those projected levels on the following day.

2. **Identify the protraction.** Review 5 recent bearish days on a 1-hour or 30-minute chart. For each day, note whether price showed a London Normal Protraction or a London Delayed Protraction between midnight and 5:00 AM NY local time. Label phases I, II, and III.

3. **New York session trade log.** For the next 5 trading days, draw a Fibonacci from London session low to high. Note when price retraced into OTE during New York session. Did a 1-minute MSS form? Would you have taken the trade?

## Recap

- ICT Intraday Profiles describe typical daily price behavior patterns, centered around specific turn times (2:00 AM, 5:00 AM, 2:00 PM, 8:00 PM NY local time).
- The Central Bank Dealers Range (CBDR) is a 6-hour range from 2:00 PM to 8:00 PM NY local time, measured using candle bodies and projectable via standard deviation.
- The London Normal Protraction Sell Profile has three phases: initial rally (midnight–2:00 AM), slow momentum protraction (2:00 AM–5:00 AM), and sell-off after the 5:00 AM high.
- London Delayed Protraction profiles begin with a move in the opposite direction before the 2:00 AM turning point, followed by protraction to 5:00 AM.
- New York session entries use Fibonacci retracement of the London range, targeting OTE levels with 1-minute MSS confirmation.
- All intraday profiles require alignment with higher timeframe bias — bearish bias for sell profiles, bullish bias for buy profiles.
